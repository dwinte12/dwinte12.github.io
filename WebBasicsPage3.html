    • Essays



    Here is a list of some of my personal favorites and well-acclaimed essays

    • Porgy and Bess and the Cold War
      • This essay discusses the use of the 1935 opera Porgy and Bess as Cold War propaganda by the United States. It analyzes the themes of the opera and how the align with broadcasted United States culture and also highlights the contradictions between the propaganda and the reality of American life, especially for Black Amercians.
    • M*A*S*H, Masculinity, and the Silver Screen
      • This essay focuses on the evolution of the first five seasons of the television show M*A*S*H. It covers episodes like "George" and "Dear Peg" to discuss the ways M*A*S*H critiqued manhood and its connections to countercultural movements.
    • Art and Resistance in Latin American Cold War dictatorships
      • This essay analyzes the works of Patricio Guzmán, Ángeles Donoso Macaya's book on resistance in photography, and Alicia Partnoy's memoir to explore the use of visual and literary imagery to capture humanity as a form of resistance against dehumanizing regimes.
    • Historiography of early American Spiritualism
      • This essay assesses the methodology of how different historians disect the history and relevance of American Spiritualism in the mid to late 19th century.
